# Account & Plans

The desktop app asks you to **sign in** the first time you open it. Your account
identifies your subscription; your actual working data (profiles, knowledge,
chats, keys) stays local either way — see
[Privacy & Data Storage](./privacy-and-storage.md).

## Sign in

On first launch you can **create an account** (email + password, confirmed with a
verification code sent to your inbox) or **sign in** to an existing one. Forgot
your password? The reset flow emails you a code.

Once signed in, the top bar shows your plan as a badge (**Basic** or **Plus**)
next to the account button. Click it any time to open the **Account** page:

![The Account page: your plan and the upgrade option](/img/features/account-plans.png)

## Basic vs Plus

The free **Basic** plan is a full workspace with caps; **Plus** removes them:

| | **Basic** | **Plus** |
|---|---|---|
| Chats (tabs) | up to 2 | unlimited |
| Domain profiles per chat | up to 2 | unlimited |
| Knowledge folders per chat | up to 3 | unlimited |
| Enabled extensions | up to 5 | unlimited |

When you hit a cap, NeatContext tells you at the point of action (for example
when clicking **+** for a third chat) and offers the upgrade.

## Upgrading, billing, downgrading

- **Upgrade to Plus** on the Account page opens checkout in your browser. Prices
  and any discount are shown on the plan card; taxes may apply at checkout.
- Once on Plus, the same page offers **Manage subscription** for billing changes.
- If you return to Basic, nothing is deleted: chats beyond the cap are **locked**
  (they keep their messages and show a lock icon; they unlock the moment you're
  back on Plus), and over-limit profiles/folders are detached from chats but
  **stay on disk**, ready to re-add. A notice lists exactly what was affected.

## Signing out

**Sign out** is at the bottom of the Account page. Signing out returns you to the
sign-in screen; your local workspace data remains on the machine.
